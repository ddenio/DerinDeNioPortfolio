About Me

My story starts in 2013, after the completion of my Bachelor's Degree in Petroleum and Natural Gas Engineering.

May 2013 New Mexico Institute of Mining and Technology Graduation

A passion for Mathematics, problem-solving, and the want to build and create outside the box lead me to pursue Engineering in College. My studies and path lead me to the completion of a Petroleum and Natural Gas Engineering Bachelor's Degree at New Mexico Tech in Socorro, New Mexico.

Throughout my tenure at New Mexico Tech, a vast and complete set of courses provided me an all-encompasing background of Math, Physics, Engineering, and various other Sciences. These included Calculus 1-3, General Physics 1-2, General Chemistry 1-2, Ordinary Differential Equations, Strength of Materials, Statics of Materials, Dynamics of Materials, along with the various core Geology and Petroleum courses. These courses provided a foundation of critical thinking and problem-solving that have followed me throughout my Post-Graduate tenure and continue to fill me with the need to grow, solve, and provide results in everything I set out to accomplish.

Post-College Oil Field Career

Soon after College I was hired on with an Oil Field Company called Crescent Directional Drilling, as an MWD (Measurement While Drilling) Field Engineer. MWD Field Engineering entailed on-site services including initial review of the job profile, functional testing and assembly of equipment, and interaction with directional driller and company personnel to ensure project objectives were met.

The location-specific Well Plan was utilized to build and program the MWD tool following locational Magnetics (MWD tool used both Accelerometers and Magnetometers). The MWD tool would then be housed in the Bottom Hole Assembly and provide directional data including both Inclination (Angle) and Azimuth (Direction) while drilling, in order to determine if the drilling path was folling the drilling plan. In addition to Inclination and Azimuth, temperature, magnetics (Dip Angle, Magnetic Strength, and Gravity), and Gamma-Ray Logging (Natural Rock Radioactivity) were logged and provided. Lastly, the analysis and interpretation of well site data and ensurance that all logs, digital data, operational data, and reports are delivered to the customer and third party representatives on time was provided.

Although my 6.5 years in the Oil Industry provided me with much growth, learning, and success; it also left me with much distress and strain. In order to provide the location services necessary, a commute of 6.5-15 hours from home was required. From here, a 'hitch', or schedule of work services would occur, lasting anywhere from 14-50 days straight, depending on the workforce available for relief. Being away from friends, family, and spouse for months at a time, as well as missing near every holiday with them, topped off with the cyclic nature of the Oil Industry, bestowed upon me a change of heart and career path.
